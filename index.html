About Me

I’m an AI Research Scientist and Machine Learning Engineer driven by a deep curiosity for the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence. My research focuses on Bayesian modeling, deep learning, and large language models (LLMs) — advancing the development of interpretable, fair, and trustworthy AI systems.

Previously, I conducted postdoctoral research at the University of Cambridge and Imperial College London, where I worked on intelligent sensing and data-driven modeling. I’m passionate about exploring emerging technologies and uncovering new insights that bridge scientific understanding with real-world impact.

Publications

Here are some of my notable publications with summaries:

  1. Divya Thekke Kanapram, Lucio Marcenaro, David Martin Gomez, Carlo Regazzoni
    Graph-Powered Interpretable Machine Learning Models for Abnormality Detection in Ego-Things Network
    Sensors, 2022
    [PDF]

    This paper proposes graph-powered interpretable ML models that combine self-awareness (SA) and collective awareness (CA) for anomaly detection in networks of intelligent agents. Using Dynamic Bayesian Networks (DBNs) and a Markov Jump Particle Filter (MJPF), interpretability is achieved through graph-based semantic matching, allowing self-explanation and incremental learning.

  2. Divya Thekke Kanapram, Fabio Patrone, Pablo Marin-Plaza, Mario Marchese, Eliane L. Bodanese, Lucio Marcenaro, David Martín Gómez, and Carlo Regazzoni
    Collective Awareness for Abnormality Detection in Connected Autonomous Vehicles
    IEEE Internet of Things Journal, 2020
    [PDF]

    Develops collective awareness (CA) models for autonomous vehicle networks, detecting system-wide anomalies using DBNs and MJPF. The study evaluates robustness under communication delays and packet losses, modeling real-world vehicular networks.

  3. Divya Thekke Kanapram, Pablo Marin-Plaza, Lucio Marcenaro, David Martin, Arturo de la Escalera, and Carlo Regazzoni
    Self-awareness in Intelligent Vehicles: Feature-Based Dynamic Bayesian Models for Abnormality Detection
    Robotics and Autonomous Systems, 2020
    [PDF]

    Introduces self-awareness models for intelligent vehicles using feature-based switching DBNs. The method detects unseen driving behaviors via low-dimensional sensor features, achieving 78% AUC accuracy in detecting anomalies.

  4. Divya Thekke Kanapram, Damian Campo, Mohamad Baydoun, Lucio Marcenaro, Eliane L. Bodanese, Carlo Regazzoni, and Mario Marchese
    Dynamic Bayesian Approach for Decision-Making in Ego-Things
    IEEE WF-IoT, 2019
    [PDF]

    Presents a Bayesian decision-making framework using DBNs and Growing Neural Gas (GNG) to enhance precision in anomaly detection. It selects optimal feature sets to improve prediction accuracy in IoT networks.

  5. PhD Thesis: Ego Things: Networks Of Self-Aware Intelligent Objects

    Develops hierarchical Dynamic Bayesian models integrating self- and collective-awareness for intelligent agents. The research combines ML, signal processing, and IoT for anomaly detection and decision-making, enhanced via graph-based interpretability.

    Access thesis online
  6. A framework to correlate power consumption and resource usage in cloud infrastructures

    Introduces the Green Cloud Abstraction Agent (GCAA), which correlates resource usage and power consumption, enabling energy-efficient VM placement using OpenStack Ceilometer and Kwapi/IPMI data.

  7. Exploring the trade-off between performance and energy consumption in cloud infrastructures

    Explores the performance–energy trade-off in cloud systems using GCAA and Kwapi frameworks, providing abstractions for energy-aware orchestration and green scheduling decisions in distributed environments.
